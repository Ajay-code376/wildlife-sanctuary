BLACK-BACKED JACKAL

The black-backed jackal is a member of the dog family.It has a rusty red coat with a
black patch on its back and a black-tipped tail.It measure around 95 centimetres(37
inches)from head to tail.

As opportunistic omnivores,jackals can eat what they want.They work in family parks to
hunt small antelopes,but they'll also feed on reptiles,insects,birds,fruits,berries and
carcasses left by larger predators.

There are two distinct populations of black-backed jackals in Africa-one in East Africa,
in countries including Kenya,Ethiopia and Tanzania and one in Southern africa in
countries including Namibia,Angola and South africa

These animals live in a wide variety of habitats,from deserts to grasslands to savannahs.
The IUNC lists black-backed jackals as least concern,meaning they face few threats.
However,they are hunted by farmers who kill them to protect their livestock.
